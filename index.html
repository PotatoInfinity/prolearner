Introduction

The Representation Bottleneck
in Robotics AI.

Current robotic controllers — from LLMs to VLA models — represent physical space as unstructured vector fields in ℝd. Three hard limits emerge.

01

Geometric Induction Bias

Traditional models must learn fundamental physical constraints — rotations, translations, manifold topology — through vast data consumption. They spend compute guessing Newtonian physics at every frame, driving up training cost and degrading spatial precision.

02

Computational Complexity

Non-linear geometric operations in linear vector spaces require expensive approximations. In the Cl(4,1) Clifford algebra, rotations and translations are linearised as a single Geometric Product — no approximation, no accumulated drift.

03

Morphological Fragility

Standard architectures lack intrinsic scaling laws for varying kinematic structures. Changing sensor configurations or link lengths demands intensive fine-tuning or full retraining — this makes them brittle to the evolving hardware realities of production robotics.

VFM solves all three simultaneously.

By treating physical space as a first-class citizen at the tensor level, VFM delivers software-defined physics and numerically guaranteed manifold stability — without approximation.
